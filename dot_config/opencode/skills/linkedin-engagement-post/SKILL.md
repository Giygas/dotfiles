# Gustavo's LinkedIn Post Guide

## Who Gustavo Is

Gustavo is a junior developer looking for his first job in France. He writes a
series of analytical, alarming posts about AI's impact on the software
development industry. He is the developer living through this — not an analyst
commenting from the outside. This personal angle is his strongest card.

When posting about his own technical projects, he shifts to a more technical
voice but keeps the same human, conversational tone.

## Input Modes

### Mode 1: Opinion / Series Post

User wants to share a take on AI, dev jobs, surveillance or tech industry.
Extract the argument, find the data, write in Gustavo's alarming/cynical voice.

### Mode 2: Technical / Project Release

User provides a version, changelog, GitHub diff or project description. Find the
narrative — not what the project does, but what decision led to it and what it
taught them.

### Mode 3: Rough Idea

User gives a theme or feeling ("I want to post about X"). Ask 2-3 targeted
questions to get the story, then write.

### Mode 4: Rewrite

User provides a draft they're unhappy with. Diagnose what's wrong first, then
rewrite.

---

## Process

### Step 1: Extract the Story

Before writing, identify:

- The hook — what is the single most surprising or uncomfortable thing here
- The tension — what problem or contradiction exists
- The insight — what the reader should feel or understand differently after
  reading
- The save-worthy element — what makes this worth bookmarking

If you don't have enough to answer these, ask. Max 2-3 targeted questions.

### Step 2: Write the Post

Write one post by default. Offer a second angle only if there's a genuinely
different framing worth presenting — not just a tone variation.

---

## Voice and Tone

- Personal, direct, slightly alarming
- Cynical but not conspiratorial — grounded in facts and data
- Conversational, not corporate
- Short punchy sentences for key moments, prose for the build-up
- Self-aware and occasionally self-deprecating ("I'm looking for my first dev
  job")
- Never preachy — state the problem, let the reader sit with it
- Write like a real person talking to a colleague, not a thought leader
  addressing an audience

**Never use:** transformative, robust, seamless, journey, excited to share,
proud to announce, game-changer

---

## Formatting Rules

- NO dashes — use commas or periods instead
- No bullet points inside the post
- Empty lines between paragraphs
- Strategic single-sentence lines for impact at key moments — once or twice per
  post maximum
- End with an open question OR a quiet unsettling closer — not both
- Hashtags at the end, 4-5 relevant ones
- No external links in the post body — add them in the first comment and
  reference it at the end of the post

---

## Structure Pattern (Opinion/Series Posts)

1. Hook — personal or with a provocative fact, max 2-3 lines, must land before
   "See More"
2. The observation or problem
3. The systemic layer — why this matters beyond the individual
4. The uncomfortable implication
5. Closer — open question or quiet statement

## Structure Pattern (Technical/Project Posts)

1. Hook — one number, one decision, or one surprising thing (max 140 characters)
2. Context — just enough background, 2-3 lines
3. What happened — the actual story, 3-5 lines
4. The insight — what this means, what you learned, make it save-worthy
5. CTA — a genuine question, not "drop a comment below"

---

## Length

- Target: 200-300 words (1,200-1,800 characters)
- Max: 350 words — go longer only if every line earns dwell time
- Shorter (150 words) is fine if everything lands
- Every paragraph must earn its place — cut anything that doesn't add a new idea

---

## Anti-AI Writing Checklist

Before finishing, scan for these patterns and rewrite:

- "That's not a X, that's a Y" — too clean and constructed
- Parallel sentence structures that feel deliberate and polished
- "Here's the part nobody talks about" — cliché opener
- "The question isn't X. It's Y." — overused format
- "Make of that what you will" — fine to keep
- "Not today. Give it a couple of years." — fine to keep
- Ending a line with a single word for drama — works once, not repeatedly
- "Doing so" / "as such" / "in this context" — AI filler, cut
- Any sentence that could appear in a press release

## LinkedIn Algorithm — What Actually Matters

### Rewards

- Comments with substance — LinkedIn reads what people say, not just that they
  commented
- Saves and bookmarks — posts people save get resurfaced for weeks, write
  something worth bookmarking
- Dwell time — how long people spend reading matters more than likes
- First hour engagement — strong early engagement determines wider reach
- Native content — posts that keep people on LinkedIn perform better than ones
  with external links
- Posting consistency — 2-3x per week compounds over time

### Penalizes

- Engagement bait ("comment YES if you agree")
- More than 5 hashtags
- Posting more than once in 12 hours
- External links in the post body — always put links in the first comment

### Best Posting Time for Gustavo

2:30pm France time (8:30am EST) — catches both European morning and US East
Coast morning simultaneously. Tuesday to Thursday is optimal but any weekday
works.

## Common Failure Modes (for Rewrite Mode)

| Symptom                                  | Fix                                                                 |
| ---------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Reads like a press release               | Find one personal moment and lead with it                           |
| Too many bullet points                   | Find the story arc, write in prose                                  |
| Stacked metrics                          | Pick one hero metric                                                |
| "Excited to share" / "Proud to announce" | Delete and start with the actual thing                              |
| Asks for engagement at the end           | Replace with a genuine question                                     |
| Too long                                 | Cut anything that doesn't serve the hook or the insight             |
| External link in post body               | Move to comments, reference in post                                 |
| Hook buried after "See More"             | Put the most compelling line in the first ~140 characters           |
| Nothing save-worthy                      | Add one actionable takeaway, counterintuitive insight, or framework |
| Sounds like AI wrote it                  | Run the anti-AI checklist above                                     |
